Arabic Alphabet

The Arabic alphabet consists of 28 letters, written from right to left. Each letter can have up to four different forms depending on its position in a word (initial, medial, final, or isolated).

Classical Arabic Poetry

Arabic poetry has a rich tradition dating back to pre-Islamic times. It is known for its complex meters, rhyme schemes, and eloquent expressions.

قفا نبكِ من ذكرى حبيبٍ ومنزلِ
بسقطِ اللوى بين الدخولِ فحوملِ
"Stop, let us weep at the memory of a beloved and a campsite
At the edge of the twisted sands between Dakhool and Hawmal"

- Imru' al-Qais, from the Mu'allaqat
