Greetings and Introductions

Learn how to greet people and introduce yourself in Arabic.

Arabic Phrase Transliteration Translation
مَرْحَبًا Marḥaban Hello / Hi
السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ As-salāmu ʿalaykum Peace be upon you (Common Islamic greeting)
وَعَلَيْكُمُ السَّلَام Wa ʿalaykumu s-salām And upon you be peace (Reply to As-salāmu ʿalaykum)
صَبَاحُ الخَيْر Ṣabāḥu l-khayr Good morning
مَسَاءُ الخَيْر Masāʾu l-khayr Good evening
كَيْفَ حَالُكَ؟ (to a male) / كَيْفَ حَالُكِ؟ (to a female) Kayfa ḥāluk(a/i)? How are you?
أَنَا بِخَيْر، شُكْرًا Anā bikhayr, shukran I am fine, thank you
مَا اسْمُكَ؟ (to a male) / مَا اسْمُكِ؟ (to a female) Mā ismuk(a/i)? What is your name?
اسْمِي... Ismī... My name is...
تَشَرَّفْنَا Tasharrafnā Nice to meet you (Literally: We are honored)
مَعَ السَّلَامَة Maʿa s-salāmah Goodbye (With safety)

Shopping and Dining

Essential phrases for when you're out shopping or at a restaurant.

Arabic Phrase Transliteration Translation
بِكَمْ هَذَا؟ Bikam hādhā? How much is this?
أُرِيدُ هَذَا، مِنْ فَضْلِكَ (to male) / مِنْ فَضْلِكِ (to female) Urīdu hādhā, min faḍlik(a/i) I want this, please
هَلْ لَدَيْكَ...؟ Hal ladayk(a/i)...? Do you have...?
أَيْنَ الحَمَّام؟ Ayna l-ḥammām? Where is the bathroom?
قَائِمَةُ الطَّعَام، مِنْ فَضْلِكَ (to male) / مِنْ فَضْلِكِ (to female) Qāʾimatu ṭ-ṭaʿām, min faḍlik(a/i) The menu, please
الحِسَاب، مِنْ فَضْلِكَ (to male) / مِنْ فَضْلِكِ (to female) Al-ḥisāb, min faḍlik(a/i) The bill, please
لَذِيذ! Ladhīdh! Delicious!

Cultural Expressions

Common expressions that reflect Arabic culture.

Arabic Phrase Transliteration Translation / Meaning
إِنْ شَاءَ الله In shāʾ Allāh If God wills (Used when talking about future events)
مَا شَاءَ الله Mā shāʾ Allāh What God has willed (Used to express admiration or appreciation, and to avoid envy)
الحَمْدُ لله Al-ḥamdu lillāh Praise be to God (Used to express gratitude or relief)
مِنْ فَضْلِكَ (to male) / مِنْ فَضْلِكِ (to female) Min faḍlik(a/i) Please (Literally: From your grace)
شُكْرًا Shukran Thank you
عَفْوًا ʿAfwan You're welcome / Excuse me
