2. Nasal Sounds

French has distinctive nasal vowel sounds that don't exist in English. The air flows through both the mouth and nose.

an/am/en/em as in "an" (year) [ɑ̃]
on/om as in "bon" (good) [ɔ̃]
in/im/ain/aim/ein as in "vin" (wine) [ɛ̃]
un/um as in "un" (one) [œ̃]

3. French Accent Marks

French uses several accent marks that change the pronunciation of letters. Here are the main ones:

Acute Accent (é)

Changes the sound to [e] as in "été" (summer)

Grave Accent (è, à, ù)

Changes the sound to [ɛ] as in "père" (father)

Circumflex (â, ê, î, ô, û)

Can indicate a historical 's' or change the vowel sound as in "fête" (party)

Diaeresis (ë, ï, ü, ÿ)

Indicates that the vowel is pronounced separately as in "Noël" (Christmas)

Cedilla (ç)

Makes 'c' sound like 's' before 'a', 'o', 'u' as in "ça" (that)

French Culture

Understanding French culture is essential for truly mastering the language. France has a rich cultural heritage that influences art, cuisine, literature, and social norms around the world.

French Cuisine

Regional Specialties

  • Provence: Ratatouille, bouillabaisse, tapenade
  • Burgundy: Boeuf bourguignon, coq au vin, escargots de Bourgogne
  • Alsace: Choucroute garnie, tarte flambée, baeckeoffe
  • Southwest France: Cassoulet, confit de canard, foie gras
  • Brittany: Crêpes, galettes, kouign-amann

Meal Structure

  • Le petit-déjeuner: Light breakfast with croissants, baguette, coffee
  • Le déjeuner: Main meal of the day, often 2+ courses
  • Le goûter: Afternoon snack (common for children)
  • Le dîner: Lighter evening meal, often served late (8-10pm)

Dining Etiquette

  • Keep hands on the table (not in lap) during meals
  • Wait for the host to say "Bon appétit" before eating
  • Bread is placed directly on the table, not on a plate
  • Cheese is served before dessert, not with crackers

Art & Literature

Art Movements

  • Impressionism: Monet, Renoir, Degas
  • Post-Impressionism: Van Gogh, Cézanne, Gauguin
  • Fauvism: Matisse, Derain
  • Cubism: Picasso, Braque

Literary Giants

  • Victor Hugo: Les Misérables, The Hunchback of Notre-Dame
  • Alexandre Dumas: The Three Musketeers, The Count of Monte Cristo
  • Albert Camus: The Stranger, The Plague
  • Marcel Proust: In Search of Lost Time

Cultural Institutions

  • Musée du Louvre: World's largest art museum
  • Musée d'Orsay: Impressionist and Post-Impressionist masterpieces
  • Centre Pompidou: Modern and contemporary art
  • Palais Garnier: Historic opera house in Paris

Customs & Etiquette

Social Greetings

  • La bise: Cheek kisses (1-4 depending on region)
  • Bonjour: Essential greeting when entering shops/businesses
  • Vous vs. Tu: Use "vous" in formal situations and with strangers
  • Handshakes are common in business settings

Social Customs

  • Politeness is highly valued (say "bonjour", "merci", "au revoir")
  • Dress neatly when going out in public
  • Maintain eye contact during conversations
  • Avoid discussing money or personal finances

Business Etiquette

  • Punctuality is important (5-10 minutes late is acceptable for social events)
  • Business cards are exchanged without formal ritual
  • Meetings may start with small talk before business
  • Decisions are often made at the top of the hierarchy

Regional Differences

Northern France

  • Climate: Cooler, more rainfall
  • Cuisine: Hearty dishes, butter-based sauces
  • Culture: More reserved, punctual
  • Dialect: Closer to standard French

Southern France

  • Climate: Mediterranean, warmer
  • Cuisine: Olive oil, garlic, herbs, seafood
  • Culture: More relaxed, "joie de vivre"
  • Dialect: Occitan influences, slower speech

Key Regions

  • Paris/Île-de-France: Fast-paced, cosmopolitan
  • Provence: Lavender fields, Mediterranean lifestyle
  • Brittany: Celtic influences, crepes, cider
  • Alsace: German influences, Christmas markets
