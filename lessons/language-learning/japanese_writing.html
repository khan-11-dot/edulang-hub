Japanese Writing Systems

Master the three Japanese writing systems: Hiragana, Katakana, and Kanji

The Three Writing Systems

Japanese uses three different writing systems, each with its own purpose and characteristics. Understanding all three is essential for reading and writing Japanese fluently.

Hiragana (ひらがな)

A phonetic alphabet used for native Japanese words and grammatical elements.

  • 46 basic characters
  • Used for particles and word endings
  • First writing system taught to children
  • Represents all sounds in Japanese
Learn Hiragana

Katakana (カタカナ)

Used for foreign words, names, and onomatopoeia.

  • 46 basic characters
  • Used for loanwords and emphasis
  • Similar structure to Hiragana
  • Sharper, more angular appearance
Learn Katakana

Kanji (漢字)

Logographic characters borrowed from Chinese, each with meaning and pronunciation.

  • Over 2,000 common characters
  • Each character has meaning
  • Multiple readings per characterEssential for reading fluency
Learn Kanji
