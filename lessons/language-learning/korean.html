Learning Tips

Korean consonants were designed to represent the shape of the mouth when pronouncing them. This makes them easier to learn and remember!

  • Initial vs Final Position: Some consonants sound different depending on their position in a syllable.
  • Aspirated Consonants: Letters like „Öã, „Öå, „Öç, and „Öä are pronounced with a puff of air.
  • Tense Consonants: Double consonants like „Ñ≤, „Ñ∏, „ÖÉ, „ÖÜ, and „Öâ are pronounced with tension in the throat.

Consonant Shape Mnemonics

Korean consonants were designed to represent the shape of the mouth when pronouncing them.

„Ñ±

g/k (giyeok)

Looks like the corner of a Game board or a Kite's corner.

The tongue touches the back of the mouth, forming a corner shape like „Ñ±.
„Ñ¥

n (nieun)

Resembles a sideways N or a nose profile.

The tongue touches the roof of the mouth, forming a shape like „Ñ¥.
„ÖÅ

m (mieum)

Looks like a square mouth when saying "mmm".

The lips close together forming a square shape like „ÖÅ.
„ÖÖ

s (siot)

Resembles a slanted roof or the top of a capital S.

The tongue position creates a narrow passage like the shape of „ÖÖ.

Vowel Pattern Memory System

Korean vowels follow logical patterns that make them easier to remember.

„Öè vs „Öì

a vs eo

Think of „Öè as pointing Away (right) and „Öì as pointing toward you (left).

The vowel sounds follow the direction: „Öè (a) points right, „Öì (eo) points left.
„Öó vs „Öú

o vs u

„Öó points Over (up) and „Öú points Under (down).

Remember: O is on top („Öó), U is underneath („Öú).
„Ö£+ Vowels

Adding „Ö£ (i)

Adding „Ö£ to a vowel creates a Y-sound version: „Öè (a) ‚Üí „Öë (ya).

Adding an extra stroke creates a Y-sound: „Öì (eo) ‚Üí „Öï (yeo).
Compound Vowels

Combining Vowels

Vowels can be combined: „Öó + „Öè = „Öò (wa).

Think of compound vowels as blending the sounds of their components.

Effective Learning Strategies

Proven techniques to help you master Hangul faster and remember it longer.

Write Each Character 20 Times

Muscle memory is powerful. Writing each character multiple times helps your brain and hand remember the shapes.

Sound-Shape Association

As you write each character, pronounce it aloud to create a strong connection between the sound and shape.

Learn in Logical Groups

Study consonants with similar shapes together („Ñ±, „Ñ¥, „Ñ∑) and vowels with similar patterns („Öè, „Öì, „Öó, „Öú).

Post-it Note Method

Label common objects in your home with Korean characters to see them throughout your day.

Switch Your Phone to Korean
