Spanish Grammar Essentials

Spanish grammar has some key differences from English. Here are some essential grammar points to master:

Grammar Point Rule Example
Noun Gender Nouns are either masculine or feminine el libro (the book, masc.), la casa (the house, fem.)
Present Tense Regular verbs end in -ar, -er, or -ir Yo hablo (I speak), Tú comes (You eat)
Ser vs. Estar Both mean "to be" but are used differently Yo soy estudiante (I am a student), Yo estoy feliz (I am happy)
Adjective Agreement Adjectives must agree in gender and number el libro rojo (the red book), la casa roja (the red house)
Subject Pronouns Often omitted as verb endings indicate the subject (Yo) hablo español (I speak Spanish)
