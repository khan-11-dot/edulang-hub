Ghazal and Nazm Forms

Urdu poetry is renowned for its beautiful forms, with Ghazal and Nazm being the most prominent:

Ghazal (غزل)

A poetic form consisting of rhyming couplets and a refrain, with each line sharing the same meter. The ghazal traditionally deals with themes of love, separation, and longing.

ہزاروں خواہشیں ایسی کہ ہر خواہش پہ دم نکلے
بہت نکلے میرے ارمان لیکن پھر بھی کم نکلے
"Thousands of desires, each worth dying for...
Many of them I have realized... yet I yearn for more..."

- Mirza Ghalib

Key Features of Ghazal:

  • 5-15 rhyming couplets (sher)
  • Radif (refrain) and Qafiya (rhyme scheme)
  • Matla (opening couplet) sets the tone
  • Maqta (final couplet) often includes the poet's pen name

Nazm (نظم)

A type of poetry that is not bound by the restrictions of ghazal and can be on any topic or theme. Nazm is more flexible in structure and content.

لَب پہ آتی ہے دُعا بن کے تمَنّا میری
زندگی شمع کی صورت ہو خُدایا میری
"My longing comes to my lips as a prayer
O God, let my life be like a candle"

- Allama Iqbal

Types of Nazm:

Hamd (حمد)

Poetry in praise of Allah

Na`at (نعت)

Poetry in praise of the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH)

Marsiya (مرثیہ)

Elegy, especially for the martyrdom of Imam Hussain

Qasida (قصیدہ)

Panegyric poetry, often in praise of kings or nobles

Famous Poets and Works

Mirza Ghalib (1797-1869)

Considered one of the most popular and influential poets of the Urdu language. His ghazals have been interpreted and sung in many different ways.

Famous Work: Diwan-e-Ghalib

Allama Iqbal (1877-1938)

National poet of Pakistan who wrote poetry in Urdu and Persian. He is known for his philosophical poetry and concept of Khudi (selfhood).

Famous Work: Bang-e-Dra, Bal-e-Jibril

Faiz Ahmed Faiz (1911-1984)

One of the most celebrated poets of the Urdu language. His poetry reflects his Marxist leanings and his opposition to oppression and tyranny.

Famous Work: Dast-e-Saba, Zindan-Nama

Literary Devices in Urdu Poetry

تشبیہ (Tashbih) - Simile

Explicit comparison between two unlike things using words like 'like' or 'as'.

چہرہ چاند کی مانند ہے
"The face is like the moon"

استعارہ (Isti'ara) - Metaphor

Implied comparison between two unlike things.

A poem written to commemorate the martyrdom of Hazrat Imam Hussain and his comrades.

A poetic form, often panegyric, written in praise of a king or a nobleman.

Cultural Context

Urdu poetry is deeply embedded in the cultural fabric of South Asia and reflects the social, political, and spiritual dimensions of the region.

Mushaira (مشاعرہ)

A poetic symposium where poets gather to present their work, often in a competitive yet respectful environment. Mushairas are an important cultural event in Urdu-speaking communities.

Qawwali (قوالی)

A form of Sufi devotional music that often features poetry from famous Sufi poets like Amir Khusrau and Bulleh Shah.

Influence on Bollywood

Urdu poetry has had a profound influence on Hindi film music, with many famous songs being direct adaptations of Urdu ghazals and nazms.

Modern Urdu Poetry

Contemporary Urdu poetry continues to evolve, addressing modern themes and issues while maintaining its traditional forms and linguistic beauty.

Famous Poetry Collections
