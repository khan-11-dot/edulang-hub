Urdu Script Mastery

Urdu is written in the Persian-Arabic script, which is written from right to left. It consists of 38 letters, most of which are derived from the Arabic and Persian alphabets, with some additional letters to represent sounds specific to Urdu.

Letter Forms

Urdu letters change shape depending on their position in a word:

ب (initial) - بـ (medial) - ـبـ (medial) - ـب (final)

Example with 'Bay':

ب + ی + ت = بیٹ (son)

ک + ت + ا + ب = کتاب (book)

Notice how the letters connect differently in different positions.

Diacritical Marks

Urdu uses diacritical marks (harakat) to indicate short vowels and other sounds:

ـَ (zabar) - a as in "cat"

ـِ (zer) - i as in "sit"

ـُ (pesh) - u as in "put"

ـّ (tashdeed) - consonant doubling

ٰ (madd) - vowel lengthening

Example: کِتاب (kitab - book)

Calligraphy Basics

Urdu calligraphy is an art form. Here are the basic strokes:

1. Vertical stroke (Alif - ا)

2. Horizontal stroke (Kaaf - ک)

3. Curved stroke (Jeem - ج)

4. Dots (Nuqta - نقطہ)

Start with practicing individual letters before connecting them.

Essential Phrases

Learn common Urdu phrases for everyday situations to help you communicate effectively in Urdu-speaking environments.

Greetings and Introductions

السلام علیکم As-salamu alaykum Hello / Peace be upon you
آپ کا نام کیا ہے؟ Aap ka naam kya hai? What is your name?
میرا نام ۔۔۔ ہے Mera naam ... hai My name is ...

Shopping and Dining

یہ کتنے کا ہے؟ Ye kitne ka hai? How much is this?
میں یہ لوں گا/لوں گی Main ye loonga/loongi I'll take this
مینو چاہیے Menu chahiye I would like the menu

Travel and Directions

یہاں کیسے جاؤں؟ Yahan kaise jaaun? How do I get there?
بائیں / دائیں Baayein / Dayein Left / Right
قریب ترین میٹرو سٹیشن کہاں ہے؟ Qareeb tareen metro station kahan hai? Where is the nearest metro station?

Urdu Poetry

Urdu poetry is known for its beauty, depth, and emotional resonance. The ghazal is one of the most popular forms of Urdu poetry, consisting of rhyming couplets with a refrain.

دل ہی تو ہے نہ سنگ و خشت، درد سے بھر نہ آئے کیوں
روئیں گے ہم ہزار بار، کوئی ہمیں سُنانے آئے
"The heart is not made of stone or brick, why wouldn't it be filled with pain?
We will cry a thousand times, if someone comes to listen to us."

- Mirza Ghalib

Urdu Calligraphy

Urdu calligraphy (خطاطی) is a beautiful art form that has been practiced for centuries. It combines artistic expression with the written word.

خوش آمدید
Nastaliq Style
The most common style used for Urdu, characterized by its hanging letters and fluid curves.
السلام علیکم
Naskh Style
A more angular and structured style, often used for religious texts.
محبت
Diwani Style
An ornate and decorative style with intricate curves and flourishes.
