Customs & Etiquette

Vietnamese social customs emphasize respect, harmony, and proper conduct in various situations.

  • Greetings: Handshake with both hands and a slight bow; use appropriate titles
  • Gift Giving: Present gifts with both hands; avoid black or white wrapping (funeral colors)
  • Body Language: Don't touch someone's head; avoid pointing with fingers
  • Table Manners: Wait to be seated; try a bit of everything; don't finish all food (suggests host didn't provide enough)
  • Business Etiquette: Punctuality is valued; exchange business cards with both hands

Regional Variations

Vietnam's long geography has led to distinct regional cultures from North to South.

  • Northern Vietnam: More traditional and formal; influenced by Chinese culture; distinct four seasons
  • Central Vietnam: Imperial heritage (Hue); spicier cuisine; more reserved culture
  • Southern Vietnam: More relaxed and open; influenced by Khmer and French cultures; tropical climate
  • Highlands: Home to ethnic minorities with distinct traditions and languages
  • Urban vs Rural: Major cities like Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City are more cosmopolitan than rural areas

Family & Social Structure

The family is the cornerstone of Vietnamese society, with Confucian values shaping social relationships.

  • Family Hierarchy: Respect for elders; father as head of household; strong extended family ties
  • Gender Roles: Traditionally defined but evolving; women often manage household finances
  • Education: Highly valued; parents make significant sacrifices for children's education
  • Social Harmony: Maintaining "face" and avoiding public confrontation is important
  • Marriage: Traditionally arranged, though love marriages are now common; elaborate wedding ceremonies
