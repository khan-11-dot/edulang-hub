Vietnamese Tones

Vietnamese is a tonal language with six distinct tones. The meaning of a word changes depending on the tone used.

Essential Vietnamese Vocabulary

Building your vocabulary is key to becoming fluent in Vietnamese. Here are some essential words and phrases organized by category:

Greetings & Basics

  • Xin chào Hello
  • Tạm biệt Goodbye
  • Cảm ơn Thank you
  • Xin lỗi Sorry/Excuse me
  • Bạn khỏe không? How are you?
  • Tôi khỏe I'm fine
  • Vâng/Không Yes/No

Numbers & Time

  • Một, hai, ba One, two, three
  • Bốn, năm, sáu Four, five, six
  • Bảy, tám, chín, mười Seven, eight, nine, ten
  • Hôm nay Today
  • Ngày mai Tomorrow
  • Hôm qua Yesterday
  • Mấy giờ rồi? What time is it?

Food & Dining

  • Cơm Rice
  • Phở Pho (noodle soup)
  • Bánh mì Baguette sandwich
  • Cà phê Coffee
  • Nước Water
  • Nhà hàng Restaurant
  • Tính tiền Bill, please

Vietnamese Culture

Understanding Vietnamese culture is essential for truly mastering the language. Here are some key aspects of Vietnamese culture:

🍜

Vietnamese Cuisine

Vietnamese cuisine is known for its fresh ingredients, minimal use of oil, and reliance on herbs and vegetables. Famous dishes include phở (noodle soup), bánh mì (sandwich), and gỏi cuốn (spring rolls).

🧧

Festivals & Traditions

Tết (Lunar New Year) is the most important Vietnamese holiday. Other significant celebrations include Mid-Autumn Festival, Hung Kings Commemoration Day, and various regional festivals throughout the year.

👪

Family & Society

Family is central to Vietnamese culture, with strong emphasis on filial piety and respect for elders. The Vietnamese language has complex pronouns that reflect age, gender, and social relationships.
